## Quotable Statistics for Marketing

### Fragmentation & Platform Overload
- **77%** of students encountered tech challenges in the past year, with over half saying it caused significant stress
- **Students use 12-15+ distinct platforms per week** (Canvas, Zoom, Gmail, Degree Audit, Library DB, textbook sites, GroupMe, etc.)
- *"Every teacher may use a different system to communicate assignments, track grades, accept assignments, and even give feedback"*

### Graduation & Retention Crisis
- Only **41%** of students graduate within 4 years
- **24%** of first-year students don't return for sophomore year
- **~33%** of students who start college don't earn a credential within 6 years
- **37%** of credits lost on average when transferring colleges

### Mental Health & Wellbeing
- **52%** of students report feeling "highly lonely"
- Only **36%** report strong personal well-being
- **37%** have moderate to severe depression symptoms
- **32%** have moderate to severe anxiety

### Financial & Basic Needs
- **65%+** have faced food or housing insecurity during college
- **67%** of students work while enrolled
- **40%** work full-time hours while enrolled
- Student debt burden topped **$1.8 trillion** in 2025

### The Non-Traditional Majority
- Only **15-16%** of undergrads live on campus
- **~22%** of undergrads are parents
- **53%** identify as commuters
- Most are commuters, working adults, parents, or first-generation students

## 10 Student Pain Points (Ranked)

### 1. "Multi-System Mayhem" - Fragmentation & Platform Overload
Students navigate too many separate systems. No unified calendar or inbox - students must manually reconcile information from LMS, email, various apps, and professor announcements.

**DormWay Solution:** Unified dashboard that aggregates all sources into one view.

### 2. "Missing in Action" - Uncoordinated Deadlines
Deadlines aren't coordinated across courses. Students experience "traffic jams" when multiple assignments cluster on the same day. Wednesday is the most common due date.

**DormWay Solution:** Academic widget showing all assignments across all courses, sorted by due date.

### 3. "Hidden Curriculum" - Administrative Friction
First-gen students often learn bureaucratic processes by trial and error. 43% of graduates took at least one unnecessary course that didn't count toward their degree.

**DormWay Solution:** Context system and Ace LLM to guide students through requirements.

### 4. "Paying the Price" - Financial Stress & Basic Needs
60-65% of students experienced food or housing insecurity during the pandemic. Working 20+ hours/week correlates with lower GPA.

**DormWay Opportunity:** Surface financial aid deadlines, campus resources.

### 5. "Communication Breakdown" - Messaging Overload
Students receive important information through too many channels. They often miss critical communications buried in email noise.

**DormWay Solution:** Unified notification feed, Inbox Actions feature.

### 6. "Mental Health Crunch" - Stress, Burnout, Isolation
~1 in 3 students struggle with significant mental health challenges. Counseling centers are overloaded with week-long wait times.

**DormWay Solution:** Proactive nudges, study/break reminders, BrainGains positive reinforcement.

### 7. "Transfer Trauma" - Credit Loss & Transition Shock
Average 37-43% of credits lost when transferring. 1 in 7 transfer students loses ALL credits.

**DormWay Opportunity:** Transfer student onboarding, credit tracking.

### 8. "Runaround for Support" - Inefficient Advising
Advisor-to-student ratios can be 1:300 or worse. Students get bounced between departments.

**DormWay Solution:** Ace LLM for 24/7 guidance, context-aware recommendations.

### 9. "Tech Troubles" - Connectivity & Device Issues
64% of students struggled with unstable internet. 29% lost connectivity during a live class or exam. 46% had device malfunctions at critical times.

**DormWay Consideration:** Offline-first architecture, progressive web app.

### 10. "Accessibility Failures" - Inclusion Gaps
Only ~5% formally disclose disabilities, but 11-15%+ have one. 18% of students need every assistive technology tool available.

**DormWay Requirement:** WCAG compliance, accessibility-first design.

## 12 Opportunity Areas (with Jobs-to-Be-Done)

### Opportunity 1: Unified Task & Calendar Hub ⭐ DORMWAY CORE
**Job:** "Always know what's due and when, across all my classes and responsibilities."

A central dashboard that automatically aggregates all assignments, exams, and important dates. Students crave a "single source of truth."

**Impact Potential:** HIGH - This directly tackles one of the most voiced frustrations.

### Opportunity 2: One-Stop Student Portal ⭐ DORMWAY CORE
**Job:** "Quickly see what I need to do today without hunting through 10 systems."

Consolidate links and notifications from all major systems with single sign-on. What students want: "a centralized environment to pay bills, check grades, register, etc., with single sign-on."

**Impact Potential:** MEDIUM-HIGH - Foundational plumbing for smoother experience.

### Opportunity 3: Proactive Advising & Degree Planning
**Job:** "Understand my path to graduation and stay on track."

Modernize degree audits into interactive planning tools. Allow "what-if" scenarios. Georgia State's approach increased graduation rates by 22 percentage points.

**Impact Potential:** VERY HIGH on retention and completion.

### Opportunity 4: Financial Wellness & Basic Needs Support
**Job:** "Balance school with work, finances, and life."

Integrate financial planning, resource referrals, and emergency support.

**Impact Potential:** HIGH for those in need.

### Opportunity 5: Seamless Communication & Notification System
**Job:** "Don't let me miss important information."

Unified notification system with student-chosen channels, AI chatbot for FAQs.

**Impact Potential:** MEDIUM - Cuts down "I didn't know" instances.

### Opportunity 6: Personal Productivity Coaching
**Job:** "Learn how to manage my time and tasks effectively."

Executive function skill-building, productivity tools with training, gamification.

**Impact Potential:** MEDIUM-HIGH for those who engage.

### Opportunity 7: Enhanced Transfer Articulation Systems
**Job:** "If I transfer, make it easy to see what will count."

National transfer credit database, algorithmic degree audits before enrollment.

**Impact Potential:** HIGH for transfer students (over 1/3 of all students).

### Opportunity 8: Mental Health & Wellness Integration
**Job:** "Support my mental health as part of my educational journey."

Embedded mental health tech, peer support networks, reduced stigma.

**Impact Potential:** HIGH on student retention and overall success.

### Opportunity 9: Accessibility-First Design
**Job:** "Make sure the system works for everyone."

Universal design, auto-captioning, simplified accommodation processes.

**Impact Potential:** EXTREMELY HIGH for disabled students, positive for all.

### Opportunity 10: Flexible & Personalized Learning Paths
**Job:** "Complete my education in a way that fits my life situation."

More flexible course delivery, modular pacing, micro-credentials.

**Impact Potential:** HIGH for adult learners, student parents, working students.

### Opportunity 11: Data-Driven Personalization (AI Tutors/Advisors)
**Job:** "Get timely, personalized help exactly when I need it."

AI tutor for 24/7 support, intelligent nudges, recommender systems.

**Impact Potential:** POTENTIALLY HIGH if done well.

### Opportunity 12: Culture & Policy Shifts
**Job:** "Make the system fair and supportive by default."

Flexible attendance, pass/fail options, streamlined bureaucracy.

**Impact Potential:** HIGH but slow to implement.

## Student Archetypes (for Personas)

### Archetype A: Residential Student (age 19)
- Lives on campus, minimal work hours
- 15 credit hours, ~18 hours studying, 5-10 hours campus activities
- Pain points: Time management, perfectionism, peer comparison
- Has most time for academics but may struggle with independence

### Archetype B: Commuter Student with Part-Time Job (age 21)
- Lives at home, 30-min commute, first-gen
- 12 credit hours, 20 hours/week work, 10 hours commute
- Pain points: Constant time crunch, ~40% of week on work+commute
- Struggles to participate in office hours or clubs

### Archetype C: Adult Learner & Parent (age 28)
- Full-time work + young child, returning student
- 6-9 credit hours (nights + online), <6 hours sleep
- Pain points: Extreme time poverty, needs asynchronous options
- Success hinges on flexible, online-friendly support

## Competitive Landscape Notes

### LMS Market (2024)
- **Canvas:** ~50% of U.S. college enrollments (leader)
- **Blackboard:** ~12% (declining)
- **D2L Brightspace, Moodle:** Trailing

### Existing Solution Gaps
1. **Unified Task/Calendar:** No widely adopted solution truly consolidates all coursework automatically
2. **Advising & Degree Tracking:** Exist but are user-unfriendly
3. **Financial Planning:** Very few tools help students forecast costs
4. **Community & Belonging:** Social apps separate from academic tools
5. **Mental Health Tech:** Therapy apps exist but stigma and efficacy questions remain

### Why Students Adopt Tools
- Directly solve acute problems
- Easy to use (few clicks)
- Work reliably
- Friends recommend them

### Why Students Abandon Tools
- Poor UX or integration (extra logins, not mobile-friendly)
- Breaks when systems update
- Requires manual setup they can't do
- Feels like extra work without payoff
