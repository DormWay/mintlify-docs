# Test Data Generation Plan: Realistic Academic Fixtures
**Date:** 2025-11-09
**Purpose:** Generate realistic test data for iOS feature parity development
**Related:** [Phase-4-iOS-Implementation-Specs](/docs/engineering/phase-4-ios-implementation-specs), [Development-Plan-Quick-Reference](/docs/engineering/development-plan-quick-reference)

---

## Executive Summary

**Problem:** Building Canvas integration, assignment completion, and notes sync **without realistic test data** is risky. We need:
- Real syllabus structures (assignment types, grading, due dates)
- Realistic schedules (time conflicts, study blocks, breaks)
- Actual note content (what students write during lectures)

**Solution:** Collect 8-12 real syllabi across diverse courses, anonymize them, and use AI to generate:
1. **Synthetic Canvas data** (courses, assignments, grades)
2. **Realistic schedules** (weekly patterns, conflicts)
3. **Sample notes** (lecture summaries, study notes)
4. **DayPlan timelines** (predicted schedules with events)

**Effort:** 8 hours (collection + anonymization + generation)

**Value:**
- Catch edge cases early (weird grading scales, unusual assignment types)
- Realistic UI testing (how does gradebook look with 40 assignments?)
- Performance testing (sync 100 assignments, 50 notes)
- Better demos (show real student workflows)

## 1. Syllabus Collection Strategy

### 1.1 Target Course Distribution

**Goal:** 8-12 syllabi covering diverse academic patterns

#### Core 4 (Must Have) - STEM Heavy

**1. Computer Science - Intro Course (CS 101)**
- **Why:** Weekly problem sets, autograded assignments, coding projects
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 10-12 weekly problem sets (100 pts each)
  - 3-4 programming projects (200 pts each)
  - 2 exams (midterm 300 pts, final 400 pts)
  - Total: ~2000 points
- **Grading:** Points-based with curve
- **Example:** "Introduction to Programming" or "Data Structures"

**2. Biology/Chemistry - Lab Science (BIO 201 or CHEM 210)**
- **Why:** Labs + lectures, lab reports, safety quizzes, group work
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 12 weekly lab reports (50 pts each)
  - 5 quizzes (20 pts each)
  - 2 exams (100 pts each)
  - 1 final project (150 pts)
  - Total: 1000 points
- **Grading:** Percentage-based
- **Example:** "Organic Chemistry I" or "General Biology with Lab"

**3. Mathematics - Calc/Stats (MATH 115 or STATS 250)**
- **Why:** Daily homework, frequent quizzes, exam-heavy
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 30+ daily homework assignments (5 pts each)
  - 10 quizzes (20 pts each)
  - 3 exams (100 pts each)
  - Total: 650 points
- **Grading:** Percentage with strict cutoffs (93% = A, 90% = A-, etc.)
- **Example:** "Calculus I" or "Intro to Statistics"

**4. English/Writing - Essay Course (ENGL 125)**
- **Why:** Major essays, peer reviews, reading responses, subjective grading
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 4 major essays (15-25% each)
  - 10 reading responses (2% each)
  - 2 peer review sessions (5% each)
  - Participation (10%)
  - Total: 100%
- **Grading:** Rubric-based, letter grades
- **Example:** "College Writing" or "Composition"

---

**5. Business - Case Study Course (BUS 300)**
- **Why:** Group projects, presentations, case analyses
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 5 case study analyses (10% each)
  - 2 group projects (15% each)
  - 1 final presentation (20%)
  - Participation (10%)
- **Grading:** Mix of individual and group grades
- **Example:** "Strategic Management" or "Marketing Principles"

**6. Psychology/Sociology - Social Science (PSYCH 111)**
- **Why:** Exams, research papers, online discussions
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 4 exams (20% each)
  - 1 research paper (15%)
  - Weekly discussion posts (5%)
- **Grading:** Percentage with partial credit
- **Example:** "Intro to Psychology" or "Sociology 101"

**7. History - Lecture Course (HIST 200)**
- **Why:** Multiple essays, primary source analyses, midterm/final
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 3 essays (20% each)
  - 2 primary source analyses (10% each)
  - Midterm (15%)
  - Final (15%)
- **Grading:** Letter grades only (no percentages shown)
- **Example:** "American History" or "World History"

**8. Art/Studio - Creative Course (ART 101)**
- **Why:** Portfolio, critiques, irregular schedule (studio time)
- **Assignment Pattern:**
  - 6 projects (portfolio pieces) (70%)
  - 3 critiques (presentations) (20%)
  - Sketchbook (10%)
- **Grading:** Pass/Fail or A-F with subjective rubrics
- **Example:** "Drawing I" or "Introduction to Design"

### 1.3 Anonymization Checklist

For each syllabus collected:

**Remove:**
- [ ] Instructor name → Replace with "Dr. Smith", "Professor Jones", etc.
- [ ] Student names (if any) → Remove entirely
- [ ] University name → Replace with "Example University" or keep generic "University"
- [ ] Semester/Year → Replace with "Fall 2024" (consistent date)
- [ ] Email addresses → Replace with "instructor@university.edu"
- [ ] Office hours specific locations → "Office: Building A, Room 123"
- [ ] Phone numbers → Remove

**Keep:**
- ✅ Course number and name (e.g., "BIO 201: Organic Chemistry")
- ✅ Credit hours
- ✅ Assignment names and point values
- ✅ Due dates (convert to relative: "Week 3, Monday")
- ✅ Grading scale
- ✅ Course policies (attendance, late work)
- ✅ Learning objectives
- ✅ Textbook information (useful for BrainGains testing)

### Step 3: Generate Canvas Data (3 hours)

**Create Generation Script:**
```python
# dormway-crews/scripts/generate_canvas_from_syllabus.py

import PyPDF2
from openai import OpenAI

def extract_syllabus_text(pdf_path):
    with open(pdf_path, 'rb') as file:
        reader = PyPDF2.PdfReader(file)
        text = ""
        for page in reader.pages:
            text += page.extract_text()
    return text

def generate_canvas_course(syllabus_text, semester):
    prompt = f"""
    Convert this syllabus into Canvas course JSON format.

    Syllabus:
    {syllabus_text}

    Generate:
    1. Course metadata (name, code, term)
    2. All assignments from syllabus with:
       - Name
       - Point values
       - Due dates (realistic spread across {semester})
       - Assignment groups (homework, exams, projects, etc.)
    3. Grading scale from syllabus

    Output valid JSON matching Canvas API structure.
    Include realistic submission data for past assignments.
    """

    response = openai.chat.completions.create(
        model="gpt-4",
        messages=[{"role": "user", "content": prompt}],
        response_format={"type": "json_object"}
    )

    return response.choices[0].message.content
```

done

## 11. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Syllabi Have PII
- **Risk:** Collected syllabi contain student names, instructor emails
- **Mitigation:** Strict anonymization checklist, manual review
- **Fallback:** Use only MIT OpenCourseWare (already public, no PII)

### Risk 2: Generated Data is Unrealistic
- **Risk:** LLM generates weird assignment names, wrong date formats
- **Mitigation:** Manual review after generation, validation scripts
- **Fallback:** Hand-craft 2-3 courses if generation fails

### Risk 3: Data Generation Takes Too Long
- **Risk:** 3 hours becomes 8 hours (script debugging, LLM issues)
- **Mitigation:** Start with Tier 1 (4 courses only), expand later
- **Fallback:** Use minimal test data for Day 1, generate more during sprint

### Risk 4: Copyright Concerns
- **Risk:** Using university syllabi violates copyright
- **Mitigation:** Use only public syllabi (MIT OCW, CC-licensed)
- **Legal:** Fair use (education, transformative, anonymized)

## 12. Alternatives Considered

### Alternative A: Manual Test Data Entry ❌
- **Pros:** Full control, no generation scripts needed
- **Cons:** 20+ hours to manually create 100+ assignments, error-prone
- **Verdict:** Too slow

### Alternative B: Use Public Canvas Sandbox ⚠️
- **Pros:** Real Canvas API, no generation needed
- **Cons:** Limited courses, no control over data, might change
- **Verdict:** Good for API testing, bad for consistent fixtures

### Alternative C: Minimal Mock Data ❌
- **Pros:** Fast (1 hour), simple
- **Cons:** Doesn't catch edge cases, unrealistic UI testing
- **Verdict:** Not sufficient for production-quality development

### Alternative D: Generate from Syllabi (RECOMMENDED) ✅
- **Pros:** Realistic data, reusable, diverse edge cases
- **Cons:** 8 hours upfront
- **Verdict:** Best ROI for quality development
