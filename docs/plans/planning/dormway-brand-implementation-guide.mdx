## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Mistake 1: "Let's Make It More Professional"
**Why it's bad**: "Professional" often means corporate, which loses authenticity

**Fix**: Ask "Would Riley say this to a friend?" Not "Would a Fortune 500 say this?"

### ‚ùå Mistake 2: Adding Features Without Context
**Why it's bad**: Feature soup overwhelms students

**Fix**: Lead with outcome ("Never miss deadlines"), THEN show mechanism

### ‚ùå Mistake 3: Generic Pain Points
**Why it's bad**: "Stay organized" could be any app

**Fix**: Be specific - "Sunday night panic," "11pm realization"

### ‚ùå Mistake 4: Forgetting Authenticity
**Why it's bad**: Loses competitive advantage

**Fix**: Every page should mention "built by a student"

### ‚ùå Mistake 5: Too Much at Once
**Why it's bad**: Students bounce if overwhelmed

**Fix**: Progressive disclosure - start simple, reveal depth

## Questions & Answers

**Q: Can I deviate from the Master Guide?**
A: Only with Riley's explicit approval. The guide exists to maintain consistency.

**Q: What if I have a new idea for messaging?**
A: Test it against the tone checklist. If it passes, propose it to Riley. If approved, update the Quick Reference.

**Q: How strict is the "banned phrases" list?**
A: Very strict. Those phrases make students roll their eyes. Don't use them.

**Q: Can I use emojis?**
A: Sparingly. Social media: yes. Landing page: minimal. In-app: only if it aids understanding (üìß, ‚úÖ, ‚ö†Ô∏è).

**Q: What if competitors start using our language?**
