# January 2026 Seed Round Strategy: Quality Over Scale

**Created:** November 5, 2025
**Target Close:** January 31, 2026
**Goal:** $1-2M seed round
**Strategy:** Prove product-market fit with engaged users, not vanity metrics

---

## Executive Summary

**The Challenge:** We have 8 weeks to build a credible fundraising story for a January 2026 seed round.

**The Trap to Avoid:** Mass marketing push ‚Üí high signups ‚Üí poor retention ‚Üí VCs see red flags

**Our Strategy:** Controlled beta with 50-100 Michigan students ‚Üí prove they LOVE it ‚Üí raise on quality signals + **Campus Connect business model** ‚Üí scale post-raise

**Why This Works:**
- Seed rounds don't require scale - they require proof of product-market fit + **clear monetization path**
- "80 students with 4.8 NPS and 70% retention" > "800 signups with 20% retention"
- **Campus Connect is a high-margin B2B marketplace disguised as a student productivity app**
- Students get 100% free product (viral growth), merchants pay for contextual access
- $12B+ TAM in campus advertising market (currently fragmented and broken)
- Protects brand reputation for post-raise growth
- De-risks the product AND the business model in VC eyes
- Sets up credible "we're ready to scale both sides" narrative

**Key Insight:** We're not raising on traction. We're raising on LOVE + **a defensible, high-margin business model that scales with network effects**.

### **Slide 7: Business Model**

**Primary Revenue: Campus Connect (B2B Merchant Platform)**

DormWay is **NOT a freemium app.** It's a **contextual sponsorship platform** that keeps the student experience 100% free while monetizing through merchant partnerships.

**How It Works:**
- Students get DormWay completely free (syllabus parsing, Canvas sync, AI assistant, all features)
- Local businesses pay to surface contextually relevant offers at moments of high intent
- Example: Coffee shop offer appears when student has 10am class and walks past at 9:25am. Or a DoorDash coupon if we can tell they need to pull an all-nighter.

**Revenue Tiers:**
- **Tier 1: Contextual Listings** ($200-500/month per location)
  - Merchant appears in relevant contextual moments
  - Basic analytics dashboard

- **Tier 2: Sponsored Positioning** ($500-1,500/month per location)
  - Priority placement in contextual suggestions
  - A/B testing capabilities
  - Student preference insights (anonymized)

- **Tier 3: Campus Deals Platform** ($1,500-3,000/month per location)
  - Full marketplace integration
  - Dynamic pricing based on demand signals
  - Closed-loop attribution tracking

**Market Sizing (Conservative):**
- 250 merchants per campus @ $500/mo avg = $1.2M ARR per campus
- Year 1: 10 pilot campuses = $12M ARR potential
- Year 3: 100 campuses = $120M ARR potential

**Why This Works:**
- Zero student friction (free = viral growth)
- Privacy-preserving (no PII shared with merchants)
- Higher CAC efficiency than social ads ($12 vs $18-27)
- 90% gross margins (pure software)

**LTV/CAC Target:** Merchant LTV >$10K, CAC <$2K = 5:1 ratio

## Risk Mitigation

### **Risk 1: Can't get to 80 beta users**

**Mitigation:**
- Expand recruitment beyond friends (post in U-M subreddit, Facebook groups)
- Offer incentives (free premium for beta users)
- Lower the bar slightly (accept 60-70 users if highly engaged)

**Fallback:** "We kept it small intentionally" still works with 50 engaged users if metrics are strong

---

### **Risk 2: Retention is lower than expected (<60%)**

**Mitigation:**
- Identify WHY users aren't returning (exit interviews)
- Fix the #1 churn reason immediately
- Focus on weekly (not daily) retention as success metric

**Fallback:** "Early retention challenges are normal for beta; here's how we're fixing it"

---

### **Risk 3: Critical bug during fundraising**

**Mitigation:**
- No deploys during critical VC meeting weeks
- Have Ethan on standby for immediate fixes
- Communicate proactively with users if issues arise

**Fallback:** "We had an incident, here's how we handled it in 2 hours" (shows operational maturity)

---

### **Risk 4: VCs want more users before investing**

**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize quality over quantity (NPS, retention)
- Show clear growth plan post-raise (how you'll get to 10K students in 6 months)
- Offer to do rolling close (raise partial round now, more later)

**Fallback:** Consider raising smaller round ($500K-$750K) from angels/Dorm Room Fund to hit milestones, then raise larger round

---

### **Risk 5: Can't close by January 31**

**Mitigation:**
- Start outreach early (mid-December)
- Run a tight process (2 weeks from intro to decision)
- Be willing to close with first solid term sheet (don't over-optimize)

**Fallback:** Extend timeline to mid-February, focus on runway management
