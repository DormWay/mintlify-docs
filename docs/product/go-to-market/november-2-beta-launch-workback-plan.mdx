## Brand Guidelines Update: "For Students, By Students"

### Core Principles (Riley-Led)

#### 1. Voice & Tone

**We sound like:**
- A helpful classmate, not a corporation
- Real talk, not marketing speak
- Encouraging but honest
- "I've been there" empathy

**We don't sound like:**
- A startup pitch deck
- A corporate press release
- An overly enthusiastic sales email
- Condescending "adulting" advice

**Example Transformations:**

‚ùå **Before:** "DormWay leverages cutting-edge AI to optimize your academic workflow"
‚úÖ **After:** "We built DormWay to help you actually stay on top of deadlines"

‚ùå **Before:** "Maximize your productivity with our revolutionary platform"
‚úÖ **After:** "Stop missing stuff. See everything in one place."

‚ùå **Before:** "Join thousands of students transforming their academic journey"
‚úÖ **After:** "Built by a student who kept forgetting assignments. Now 6,000+ use it."

#### 2. Messaging Hierarchy

**Always lead with:**
1. Real student problem (relatable, specific)
2. How we solve it (simple, clear)
3. Built by students (authenticity, trust)

**Never lead with:**
1. Technology/AI features
2. Awards or accolades
3. Investment or company milestones
4. "Revolutionary" claims

#### 3. Visual Brand

**Student-First Aesthetics:**
- Clean, minimalist (students hate clutter)
- Dark mode by default (late-night studying)
- Real campus photos (not stock imagery)
- Riley's face in founder content (builds trust)

**Avoid:**
- Corporate stock photos of "students"
- Overly polished marketing visuals
- Generic office/tech aesthetics
- Anything that feels "selling to students" vs. "built for students"

#### 4. Content Approval Checklist (RileyGPT Standard)

**Before publishing any content, verify ALL of these:**

**The Riley Test (Primary):**
- [ ] Would Riley say this to a friend?
- [ ] Does this sound like a real student wrote it?
- [ ] Is it friendly first, credible second, never corporate?

**RileyGPT Style Mechanics:**
- [ ] **Clear one-sentence point up top** ‚Äî what's the main thing?
- [ ] **Proof with specifics** ‚Äî numbers, dates, examples (receipts)
- [ ] **Concise sentences** ‚Äî average 10-15 words, some shorter for punch
- [ ] **Scannable bullets/headers** ‚Äî can you skim and get it?
- [ ] **One clear CTA** ‚Äî what should they do next?
- [ ] **Jargon trimmed or explained** ‚Äî would a freshman understand this?
- [ ] **Exactly one humanizing touch** ‚Äî em dash, parenthetical aside, or light joke

**Content Structure (Why ‚Üí So What ‚Üí Now What):**
- [ ] **Why:** Context and importance established
- [ ] **So what:** Impact clearly stated
- [ ] **Now what:** Next steps or CTA obvious

**Voice Alignment:**
- [ ] Problem-first (not feature-first)
- [ ] Specific details (not generic claims)
- [ ] Plain English with active verbs
- [ ] No corporate jargon or buzzwords

**If ALL boxes checked ‚Üí Publish**

**If ANY box unchecked ‚Üí Edit and re-check**

**See full RileyGPT specifications:** `Voice-Guidelines-For-Students-By-Students.md`

#### 5. Prohibited Words/Phrases

**Never use:**
- "Leverage" (just say "use")
- "Ecosystem" (say "platform" or "app")
- "Synergy" (delete entirely)
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" (overused, unbelievable)
- "Empower" (condescending, corporate)
- "Cutting-edge" (marketing speak)
- "Transform your academic journey" (too grandiose)
- "Unlock your potential" (eye-roll territory)

**Use instead:**
- "Actually helps you..."
- "Built because we needed it"
- "Stop missing stuff"
- "See everything in one place"
- "From a student who gets it"

## Contingency Plans

### Technical Issues

**Scenario: API down or severe performance degradation**
- **Detection:** Monitoring alerts, Sentry errors spike
- **Response:**
  1. Put maintenance page up immediately
  2. Post on social: "We're seeing amazing response! Servers catching up. Back in 30 min."
  3. Scale infrastructure (see CLAUDE.md in dormway-platform for commands)
  4. Resume when stable
- **Communication:** Transparent, student-friendly tone ("We broke our own servers with hype!")

**Scenario: Onboarding bug blocking signups**
- **Detection:** Low activation rate, user complaints
- **Response:**
  1. Identify and fix bug ASAP (Ethan leads)
  2. Email affected users when fixed
  3. Offer "sorry for the trouble" bonus (early access to feature, swag)
- **Communication:** Own the mistake, share the fix timeline

### Content Issues

**Scenario: Press coverage is negative/critical**
- **Response:**
  1. Don't panic or react defensively
  2. Assess if criticism is valid
  3. If valid: Acknowledge, commit to improvement
  4. If unfair: Provide data/context, student testimonials
- **Communication:** Riley leads response (student voice builds trust)

**Scenario: Low social engagement**
- **Response:**
  1. Activate ambassadors with "help us share" message
  2. Create more relatable/vulnerable content (Riley's real struggles)
  3. Engage directly with student influencers
  4. Post behind-the-scenes, authentic content

### Messaging Issues

**Scenario: Brand voice slips into corporate speak**
- **Detection:** Riley flags content during review
- **Response:**
  1. Pause and rewrite before publishing
  2. Re-review brand guidelines with team
  3. Reinforce "Riley test" approval process
- **Prevention:** All content goes through Riley final review
