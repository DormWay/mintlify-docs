## Executive Summary

DormWay's native roadmap voting and feature request system gives students **direct control over product development**. Students can vote on upcoming features, submit their own ideas, and see real-time results‚Äîno corporate middleman, no external platform. This is student democracy in action: **your votes directly shape what we build next**.

**Core Philosophy:** Traditional software companies decide what students need. We let students decide what students want.

---

## The "By Students, For Students" Vision

### What Makes This Different

Most EdTech companies say they "listen to students." We **give students the steering wheel**.

| Traditional EdTech | DormWay Roadmap Voting |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Anonymous surveys once a year | Real-time voting every day |
| Product managers decide priorities | Student votes decide priorities |
| Feedback goes into a black hole | Public roadmap shows what's coming |
| No transparency on what's being built | Full visibility into development plans |
| Feature requests via support tickets | Dedicated public feature request board |
| Students never know if ideas were heard | Vote counts update live, Linear integration auto-creates issues at 25+ votes |

### Core Principles

1. **Radical Transparency**
   Students can see exactly what we're building, when we expect to ship it, and why we prioritized it. No hidden agendas, no corporate secrets.

2. **Democratic Prioritization**
   The most-voted features rise to the top. We don't ignore small-but-passionate groups, but we also won't build for one person at the expense of thousands.

3. **Student Ownership**
   This is YOUR product. You pay (or will pay) for it, you use it daily, you should decide how it evolves.

4. **Accountability**
   When students vote for a feature and we build it, we notify everyone who voted. When we decide NOT to build something, we explain why publicly.

5. **No External Dependencies**
   We built this ourselves instead of using Canny.io or ProductBoard. Why? Because third-party platforms limit what we can do, cost monthly fees, and put a layer between us and students.

## Technical Excellence

### Why We Built It In-House

**‚ùå Canny.io Alternative (what we didn't choose):**
- Cost: $50-400/month recurring
- External dependency
- Limited customization
- Data lives on their servers
- Generic UI that doesn't match our brand
- Can't integrate deeply with our workflows

**‚úÖ Native DormWay System (what we built):**
- Cost: $0 recurring (one-time development)
- Full control over features and UX
- Real-time updates via Ably (<100ms latency)
- Data in our PostgreSQL database
- Seamless Clerk authentication (no separate login)
- Direct Linear integration (auto-creates issues)
- RLS security policies (enterprise-grade)
- Customizable for DormWay-specific use cases

### Technical Highlights Students Care About

**Real-Time Everything:**
"Vote on your laptop, see the count update on your phone instantly. No refresh needed. No delays. Ably pub/sub makes votes feel live, like a group chat."

**Security & Privacy:**
"Row-Level Security (RLS) policies ensure you only see your own votes in the database. Your voting history is private. Public vote counts, private voter identity."

**Graceful Degradation:**
"If Ably goes down, voting still works‚Äîyou just won't see real-time updates until it's back. We never block core functionality for optional features."

**Mobile-First Design:**
"Built in Next.js with responsive design. Vote on your phone between classes, submit feature requests from your laptop at the library, check roadmap on your iPad."

## Student Empowerment Narratives

### Story 1: The Feature That Almost Didn't Happen

**Scenario:**
A sophomore at Michigan submits: "Add grade calculator to track GPA across semesters."

**Old Way (No Voting System):**
- Submits via support chat
- Gets "we'll consider it" response
- Never hears back
- Feature never built (too low on internal priority list)

**New Way (With Voting):**
- Submits feature request at `/feedback/submit-feature`
- Shares link in GroupMe with 50 friends
- Request gets 73 votes in 48 hours
- Auto-creates Linear issue (DORM-XXX: Grade calculator)
- Team reviews, adds to Q1 2026 roadmap
- Ships 6 weeks later
- All 73 voters get notification: "Your requested feature is live!"
- Sophomore becomes campus DormWay evangelist

**Result:** Student voice ‚Üí Actual feature. Not a token gesture‚Äîa genuine product development loop.

### Story 2: Transparency Wins Trust

**Scenario:**
100 students vote for "Add housing marketplace (Craigslist for dorms)"

**Old Way:**
- Company silently decides not to build (legal liability, moderation costs)
- Students wonder why their feedback was ignored
- Trust erodes

**New Way:**
- Team posts public update on feature request:
  > "We love this idea, and 100 votes shows real demand. However, marketplace features require legal review, moderation infrastructure, and liability insurance we're not equipped for yet. We're tabling this for 2026 H2 when we have the resources to do it right. Thanks for the feedback‚Äîthis helped us understand what you want, even if we can't deliver it today."
- Students appreciate honesty
- Trust increases

**Result:** Transparency > Empty promises. Students respect real constraints explained openly.

### Story 3: Small Feature, Big Impact

**Scenario:**
Only 8 students vote for "Add dark mode toggle on schedule page"

**Old Way:**
- Too few votes, gets ignored
- Those 8 students (night owls with eye strain) keep struggling

**New Way:**
- Team notices this is low-effort, high-impact
- Ships in 2 days despite low vote count
- Posts update: "Low votes, but we heard you‚Äîdark mode is live!"
- Those 8 students feel seen and valued
- They tell friends: "DormWay actually listens, even to small groups"

**Result:** Not everything is a popularity contest. We balance votes with effort/impact analysis.

## FAQ for Students

**Q: Does every feature with 25 votes get built?**
A: Not automatically, but it gets reviewed by our team and added to the backlog. We consider: technical feasibility, alignment with vision, effort required, and cross-platform impact. But 25 votes is a strong signal we take seriously.

**Q: Can I vote on multiple features?**
A: Yes! Vote on as many as you want. Your votes help us prioritize.

**Q: What if my feature idea is a duplicate?**
A: Search before submitting! If it exists, upvote it instead. Consolidating votes makes features more likely to ship.

**Q: How do I know if my voted feature shipped?**
A: We'll notify you via email and in-app notification. You can also check the Changelog.

**Q: Can I change my vote?**
A: Yes, click the button again to remove your vote. Vote freely!

**Q: Do votes from freshmen count the same as seniors?**
A: Yep. One student = one vote, regardless of year, campus, or account age.

**Q: What if I submit a feature and it gets zero votes?**
A: That's valuable data too! It tells us the idea doesn't resonate broadly (though we might still build it if it's low-effort and we see strategic value).
